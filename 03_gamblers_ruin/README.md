# Gambler's Ruin
A simulation-based exploration of the Gambler's Ruin problem, investigating how starting wealth and win probability determine survival chances, and why even a fair game makes ruin near-certain against a sufficiently wealthy opponent.

## Concepts Used
* Markov Chains
* Monte Carlo Simulation
* Ruin Probability (Closed-Form Solution)
* Expected Game Duration

## Questions Explored
* What is the probability of survival given starting wealth k out of total N?
* Why does even a fair game give only a k/N chance of winning?
* How does a small unfair edge change ruin probability so drastically?
* How long does a typical game last before one player is ruined?
