# Central Limit Theorem
A simulation-based exploration of the Central Limit Theorem across three source distributions: exponential, uniform, and normal. 
Investigating why sample means converge to a normal distribution regardless of the shape of the underlying population, and how quickly that convergence happens.

## Concepts Used

* Central Limit Theorem
* Sampling Distributions
* Standard Error (σ/√n)
* Source Distribution Comparison (Exponential, Uniform, Normal)
* Monte Carlo Simulation
* Law of Large Numbers

## Questions Explored

* Why do sample means form a normal distribution regardless of the source distribution's shape?
* How large does n need to be before convergence to normality becomes visible?
* Why does a skewed source distribution require a larger sample size than a symmetric one?
* How does standard error change as sample size increases, and does it depend on the source distribution?
