# Random Walks
A simulation-based exploration of the Random Walk problem, investigating how 
displacement grows with the number of steps and why the distribution of final 
positions converges to a normal curve regardless of the underlying step distribution.

## Concepts Used

* Markov Processes
* √n Scaling Law / RMS Displacement
* Monte Carlo Simulation
* Law of Large Numbers
* Central Limit Theorem

## Questions Explored

* Does the walker always end up near the origin after n steps?
* Why does typical displacement grow as √n and not stay near 0?
* Why do the final positions of many independent walks form a normal distribution?
* How quickly does the simulated RMS displacement converge to the theoretical √n?
